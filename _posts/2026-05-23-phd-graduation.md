My PhD was conferred recently. I'd like to share the first paragraph of the Acknowledgments section of my dissertation. It starts with an epigraph:

<div dir="rtl" lang="he">
*"מה נוסיף ונמנה? מדברים פעוטים /
  נוצרות אגדות זה החומר"*
<br>
"מסביב למדורה" / נתן אלתרמן
</div>
<br>

My academic journey so far has been somewhat non-standard; rather than a straightforward path, I have been going up and down, back and forth. Even before my PhD I had already gone quite a long way, so when I started my PhD I expected it to be relatively smooth and straightforward. Of course, this was not the case, and at times I was unsure whether I would finish what I started. I often think of my life in terms of a television series; through this perspective, the story arc of my PhD lasted four and a half seasons and included an abundance of highlights: dramatic moments, long days of extraneous work, new friendships, personal growth, tackling of inner demons (via struggle, acceptance, or letting go), dealing with health issues, impostor syndrome, various means of escapism (learning mathematics, reading and writing prose, working out, hanging out with friends and family), and of course—small moments of revelation, of scientific discovery, of elevating oneself above and beyond the day-to-day muck, of trying to forge something new and lasting.

***

Then I proceed to thank the people who acted as main characters on my show, and therefore shared those highlights with me (for more than three pages). I will not reiterate the entire Section here, but it suffices to say that I owe a great many thanks to my supervisor Eli, my colleagues, collaborators, and many wonderful friends and loving family members.

Looking forward to the next season, and to any new challenge, obstacle, adventure, and characters it may bring :)
