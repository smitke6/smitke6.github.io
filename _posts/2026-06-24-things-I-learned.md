Things I learned tonight:
1. If your fingers burn after cutting spicy peppers, washing them in water won't help because capsaicin is insoluble in water.
2. To relieve the burn, washing your hands with oil or alcohol may help, since capsaicin is soluble in both (although neither of those helped me).
3. A dull knife crushes the pepper and sprays much more capsaicin on your hand, compared to a clean cut by a sharp knife.
4. The burning sensation can last up to 24 hours if left untreated.
5. To use IKEA's knife sharpener properly, one should fill it with water (BELIEVE ME, I WAS AS SHOCKED AS YOU ARE).
6. Milk contains a protein called casein, which naturally breaks down capsaicin.
7. Apparently, Remilk's "New Milk" does *not* contain casein.
