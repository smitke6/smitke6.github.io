**At the main gate of the Weizmann Institute of Science**

**Me:** Hi, I’m auditing a course here.  
**Guard:** Let me see your pass, so I can be sure you’re not a terrorist.  
**Me:** I don’t have one. Auditors don’t get passes.  
**Guard:** Sorry, can’t let a terrorist in.  
**Me:** Oh come on, it’s the same story every week… I swear, I’m not a terrorist!  
**Guard:** *(Sighs)* Which department?  
**Me:** Math.  
**Guard:** Building?  
**Me:** Goldsmith.  
**Guard:** Classroom?  
**Me:** Is that really necessary? Why would a terrorist bother—  
**Guard:** *Which classroom?!*  
**Me:** I’m not sure! 108, I think?  
**Guard:** What’s the course called?  
**Me:** Topological K-Theory.  
**Guard:** What’s that?  
**Me:** … Do you know what a vector bundle is?  

---

*I pull a strip of paper from my pocket, twist it, and tape it together. I spend the entire morning at the gate explaining manifolds, cohomology, the Eilenberg–Steenrod axioms, and Alexander Grothendieck’s entire amazing life story.*

---

**Guard:** Thank you so much. That was absolutely fascinating.  
**Me:** So... do you believe I’m not a terrorist now? Can I finally go in?  
**Guard:** Yeah, sure, no problem man. Just open your bag for me please.  

*I unzip my backpack. The guard looks inside. Sitting at the bottom are seven or eight crumpled Möbius strips from every previous week of the semester.*
