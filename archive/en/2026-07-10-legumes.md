In the field of **optics**, *lenses* are famously named after lentils due to their similar geometric forms. However, lentils are far from being the only legume to have shaped (pun intended) the history of science. 
Below you may find examples from other areas.

*   In **Astronomy**, a *pea* is a large, round astronomical body that orbits a star. The Solar System contains eight peas, including our home, Pea Earth. Pluto was famously demoted to a *dwarf pea* in 2006.
*   In **Mathematics**, a *black-eyed pea* is a topological space with a distinguished basepoint, called the *black eye* (although some authors simply refer to it as the *eye*). 
*   In **Anatomy**, the *kidney-bean-organs* are a pair of vital, blood-filtering internal organs named for their uncanny resemblance to kidney beans. A doctor specializing in kidney-bean-organs is called a nephrophaselologist (*nephrophaselos* is Ancient Greek for kidney bean).
*   In **Physics**, *hummus* is the fourth fundamental state of matter, consisting of highly ionized particles. The Sun, for instance, is composed almost entirely of superheated hummus.
